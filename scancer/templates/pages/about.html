About the Scancer Project

Scancer is a collaborative effort to increase access to the early detection of breast cancer.

Breast cancer is the most diagnosed cancer among women worldwide, accounting for 1 in 4 cancer cases. In 2020, there were an estimated 684,996 deaths from breast cancer, with a disproportionate number of these deaths occurring in low-resource settings. [1]

Survival rates for breast cancer are very high when the cancer is detected early and where treatment is available. Unfortunately, 50 to 80% of breast cancer cases are diagnosed at an advanced stage in many low- and middle-income countries, when the cancer is usually incurable. [1]

One of the primary reasons for this is that the tests and procedures used to diagnose this disease (such as mammography and lymph node biopsy) require a lot of human expertise, which is very limited. Fortunately, most of these diagnostic procedures involve visual inspection of scans, which is ideally suited to the strengths of deep-learning-based computer vision techniques.

The specific procedures that are used to diagnose breast cancer [2] are depicted in the following graphic, and the ones that would most benefit from computer vision are denoted in purple. [3]

There are many groups that realise this, and so there exist numerous published papers that describe how deep learning models help improve different aspects of diagnosis. This exactly what the Scancer project aims to do.

How Do We Propose to Do This?

The path we have chosen relies on the following key components:

  1. A web app (written in Django) that sits within a clinically-relevant setting and improves human expert workflows.
  2. This web app wraps an API (powered by TorchServe) that serves up state-of-the-art computer vision models relevant to varying stages of breast cancer detection.
  3. A well-maintained catalogue of the best deep learning vision models for different parts of the diagnosis chain (pretrained, optimised and ready for serving).
  4. Release all the pieces open source (MIT License) and completely reproducible as possible, so that anyone can use and contribute to the effort.
